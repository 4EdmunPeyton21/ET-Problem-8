INCIDENT REPORT — January 2024
Facility: Unit A Process Plant
Equipment: PUMP-101 (Centrifugal Pump, Cooling Water Circuit)

Summary:
On 2024-01-14, operators reported abnormal vibration and rising bearing
temperature on PUMP-101. A vibration reading of 11.8 mm/s RMS was recorded
against a normal baseline of 3.5 mm/s RMS. Bearing temperature reached 92°C.

Investigation:
Maintenance technician J. Alvarez inspected the pump on 2024-01-15. Findings
indicated insufficient lubrication at the drive-end bearing, with visible
discoloration consistent with overheating. No signs of cavitation or
misalignment were found on initial inspection.

Root Cause:
Insufficient lubrication due to a missed entry on the monthly lubrication
schedule. The last recorded lubrication event was 47 days prior, exceeding
the 30-day preventive maintenance interval for this equipment class.

Corrective Action:
- Performed emergency relubrication of drive-end and non-drive-end bearings.
- Replaced worn bearing seal.
- Restored vibration to 3.9 mm/s RMS and bearing temperature to 58°C.

Preventive Measures:
- Added PUMP-101 to the automated lubrication reminder system.
- Scheduled quarterly vibration analysis for all Unit A centrifugal pumps.
- Updated procedure "Monthly Lubrication Check" to include a supervisor
  sign-off step to prevent missed intervals.

Related Equipment: MOTOR-204 (drive motor, same skid, no anomalies found)
Severity: HIGH
Status: RESOLVED
